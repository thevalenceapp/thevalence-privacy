Privacy First

The Valence is designed with privacy at its core. Most recovery data is stored locally on your device. When you use AI-powered features, selected data is sent for processing as described below. This policy describes what data we collect, how it is used, and your rights regarding that data.

Data Storage

Most personal data — including journal entries, voice memos, mood logs, reason wall items, recovery progress, and app settings — is stored locally on your device using on-device persistence. We do not maintain user accounts or a server-side database of your recovery data.

AI-Powered Features and Data Processing

The Valence uses Google's Gemini API to provide the following AI-powered features: AI Mentor, Journal AI Insight, Voice Analysis, Daily Protocol, and Command Center (Weekly Report).

When you use any of these features, the following categories of data may be sent to Google Gemini for processing:

  • Content you create — such as messages, journal text, voice recordings, and recent conversation context when needed
  • Recovery context — such as your no-contact day count, streak, mood, stability score, urge/risk level, and check-in signals
  • Activity statistics — such as SOS usage, no-contact breaches, and journal, article, or voice activity counts
  • Personalization details — such as your first name if entered, selected coach, and active focus

AI requests are routed through Cloudflare Workers and processed by Google Gemini. Valence does not store AI inputs in its own app database.

How each AI feature uses your data

AI Mentor

When you send messages to the AI Mentor, your message text and recent conversation context are sent to Google's Gemini API. The AI generates a response based on your message and your current recovery context (such as your no-contact day count, stability score, and mood). Your name may be included in the request to personalize coaching.

Journal AI Insight

When you request an AI analysis of a journal entry, the text content of that specific entry, along with previous entries for comparison, is sent to Google's Gemini API for emotional pattern analysis. The AI returns a structured analysis (emotional pattern, risk assessment, insight, and suggested action) which is then stored locally on your device alongside the entry.

Voice Analysis

When you record a voice memo and the AI analysis runs, the audio recording is sent to Google's Gemini API for transcription and emotional analysis. The resulting transcript, mood assessment, and risk score are stored locally on your device. A visible recording indicator is displayed at all times during voice recording.

Daily Protocol

When you use the Daily Protocol's AI features, recovery context (such as your mood, stability score, streak, check-in data, and recent activity statistics) is sent to Google's Gemini API for personalized coach guidance. Results are cached locally on your device.

Command Center (Weekly Report)

When you generate an AI report in the Command Center, aggregated recovery data (such as mood patterns, check-in counts, urge levels, SOS usage, no-contact breach count, journal/voice/article counts, and active focus) is sent to Google's Gemini API. Results are cached locally on your device.

All AI processing is performed by Google's Gemini API. Google's handling of this data is governed by Google's API Terms of Service and their Privacy Policy.

User Consent

A one-time AI Data Sharing consent screen is shown during the onboarding tour before the user reaches the main app. The screen discloses which AI features send data, what categories of data may be sent, and that data is routed through Cloudflare Workers and processed by Google Gemini. If the user declines or skips the tour, the same consent dialog reappears when opening any AI-enabled feature.

You must tap "Allow AI Processing" to use AI features. If you tap "Not Now," no data is sent and AI features remain disabled. Non-AI features (journaling, no-contact tracking, mood logging, articles) continue to work fully.

You can manage your AI data sharing preference at any time from the Settings screen within the app.

Voice Recordings

The Valence includes a voice recording feature for personal voice memos. In accordance with applicable requirements:

  • A clearly visible recording indicator (animated button and status label) is displayed whenever a recording is in progress.
  • Voice recordings are stored locally on your device.
  • If you choose to use AI analysis, the audio data is sent to Google's Gemini API for transcription and analysis, then the results are stored locally.
  • You can delete any voice recording at any time from within the app.

Subscriptions

The Valence offers optional premium subscriptions managed through Apple's App Store and RevenueCat. When you subscribe:

  • Payment is processed entirely by Apple. We do not collect or store your payment information.
  • RevenueCat receives an anonymous user identifier to manage your subscription status. No personal information is shared with RevenueCat.
  • Subscriptions auto-renew unless cancelled at least 24 hours before the end of the current period.
  • You can manage or cancel your subscription at any time in your device's App Store settings.

Third-Party Services

The Valence uses the following third-party services:

  • Google Gemini API — AI feature processing. Data is transmitted only when you explicitly use AI features. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Cloudflare Workers — API relay for infrastructure security between the app and Google Gemini.
  • RevenueCat — subscription management using an anonymous user identifier. No personal information is shared. See RevenueCat's Privacy Policy.
  • Firebase (Google) — backend infrastructure and app performance services. The app does not use Firebase Analytics, Firebase Crashlytics, or any Firebase user tracking features. See Firebase Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts — used for typography rendering. Font files are bundled with the app.
  • Apple App Store / TestFlight — used for app distribution and beta testing. Subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.

All third-party providers used by The Valence follow industry-standard privacy and security practices. No advertising SDKs, no cross-app tracking, and no behavioral profiling tools are used.

What We Do NOT Collect

We want to be explicit about what we do not do:

  • We do not require account creation, login, or registration.
  • We do not collect your email address, phone number, or any contact information.
  • We do not track your location or access your contacts, photos, or calendar.
  • We do not use any permanent device-based identifiers to track you.
  • We do not use advertising identifiers or tracking pixels.
  • We do not sell, share, or transfer any data to third parties for advertising, marketing, or profiling purposes.
  • We do not use cookies or web-based tracking mechanisms.

Data Retention and Deletion

Since most data is stored locally on your device:

  • You can delete individual entries (journal, voice memos, reason wall items) at any time from within the app.
  • You can reset all app data using the "Reset All Data" option in Settings, which permanently erases all locally stored information.
  • Removing the app removes its locally stored app data from the device.
  • Data sent to Google's Gemini API for processing is handled according to Google's data retention policies.

Data Security

All data stored on your device benefits from the security protections provided by your device's operating system, including device encryption. Communications with external APIs use HTTPS encryption. We recommend that you protect your device with a passcode or biometric authentication to further secure your data.

The Valence is intended for users aged 12 and older. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 12. If you believe a child under 12 has used the app, please contact us.

Your Rights (KVKK / GDPR)

If you are located in Turkey or the European Economic Area, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data (all data is already visible to you in the app).
  • Delete your data at any time using the "Reset All Data" option in Settings.
  • Withdraw consent for AI processing from the Settings screen or by simply not using AI features.
  • Request information about how your data is processed by contacting us.

Since all personal data is stored locally on your device and we do not maintain user accounts or databases, most data rights are automatically fulfilled by the app's design.
